Fanta is a brand of fruit-flavored carbonated soft drinks created by The Coca-Cola Company. Fanta originated as a Coca-Cola substitute during the American trade embargo of Nazi Germany, which affected the availability of Coca-Cola ingredients, in 1941. Fanta was originally created as a result of a shortage of Coca-Cola syrup in Nazi Germany.
